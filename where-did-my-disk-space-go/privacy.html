Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 4, 2026

Where Did My Disk Space Go? is a macOS utility for reviewing local disk usage. The app is designed to work on device.

Data collection

The app does not collect, transmit, sell, rent, or share personal data. It does not use analytics SDKs, advertising SDKs, tracking SDKs, or tracking domains.

Local file information

To show disk usage, the app reads local file metadata such as file names, paths, sizes, folder locations, and modification dates for folders it is allowed to access. This information is processed locally on your Mac and is not sent to us.

Folder access

The app may request access to folders such as Desktop, Documents, Downloads, Movies, Music, Pictures, or files and folders you select. Access is used only to display disk usage, identify cleanup candidates, reveal items in Finder, and move selected items to Trash.

Cleanup

When you choose to clean up selected items, the app moves those items to Trash. You can recover items from Trash until you empty it.

Required reason APIs

The app uses Apple disk-space APIs to display storage information and file-timestamp APIs to show file dates. These uses are declared in the app privacy manifest included in the app bundle.

Children's privacy

The app does not knowingly collect data from children or any other users.

Changes

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new effective date.

Contact

For privacy or support questions, use the support options listed at Support.
